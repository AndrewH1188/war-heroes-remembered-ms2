Remembering our War Heroes

Private Richard Spearink
25820 - Royal Fusiliers (London Regiment)
Killed 22nd March 1918 aged 34.
It was said Richard was missing but records show he was buried by German soldiers and was later reburied in St Souplet British Cemetery France.

Private Frederick Spearink
11945 - Hampshire Regiment 2nd Bn.
Killed: 13th August 1915 aged 31.
Frederick was on the HMT Royal Edward when it was torpedoed by the Germans and drowned. Commemorated on the Helles Memorial in Turkey and Mitcham War Memorial Surrey.

Lance Sergeant Thomas Banister
14690 - The King’s (Liverpool Regiment)
Killed 9th September 1915.
Buried at: Rue-Petillon Military Cemetery, Fleurbaix. Awarded Medals: 1914/15 Star, British War Medal, Victory Medal

Second Lieutenant Sidney Vandyke Hasluck
732 - 14th King George’s Own Ferozepore Sikhs, Indian Army
Killed: 4th June 1915 aged 26.
Killed in action near Krithia, Gallipoli, Dardanelles. Commemorated on the Helles Memorial in Turkey.

Nurse Elizabeth McMath (Daisy) Warnock
Voluntary Aid Detachment
Died: 5th May 1918 aged 31.
uried or commemorated at St. Sever Cemetery, Rouen France. I have finished the work which thou gavest me to do.

Private Thomas Phillips
622141 - Saskatchewan Regiment 1st Canadian Mounted Rifles
Killed: 6th June 1916 aged 34.
Reported missing presumed to have died. Commemorated: Ypres (Menin Gate Memorial) Panel 30,32 as well as on the Huthwaite Cemetery Cenotaph.

Leading Seaman John Sexton
J24832 - Royal Navy
Killed: 2nd July 1919 aged 23.
Killed when a mine exploded during mine sweeping operations in North Russia on board H.M.S. Fox. Buried / commemorated at Plymouth Naval Memorial

Lance Corporal Arthur Norman (Barney) Allen
3002 - 58th Infantry Battalion, AIF
Killed in action 29th September 1917.
No known grave as no remains found. Memorials: Menin Gate Memorial (Commonwealth Memorial to the Missing of the Ypres Salient).

Major and acting Lieutenant-Colonel Harry Moorhouse
1st/4th battalion King’s Own Yorkshire Light Infantry (KOYLI)
Killed: 9th October 1917 aged 48.
Remembered on Tyne Cot Memorial Panel 108. Awarded Medals: 1914-15 Star, British War Medal, Victory Medal.

Captain Ronald Moorhouse
1st/4th battalion King’s Own Yorkshire Light Infantry (KOYLI)
Killed: 9th October 1917 aged 22.
Remembered on Tyne Cot Memorial Panel 108. Awarded Medals: British War Medal, Military Cross, Victory Medal.

Private John “Jack” Henry Thomas
26th Canadian Infantry Battalion (New Brunswick), CEF
Killed: 19th August 1917 aged 28.
Remembered on the Canadian National Vimy Memorial and reburied in Loos British Cemetery Plot XVIII, Row H, Grave 14.

Private Bertram Alec Reader
London Regiment (Prince of Wales' Own Civil Service Rifles)
Killed: 15th September 1916 aged 18.
Killed during the Battle for High Wood, just two weeks before he was due to return to England as he was underage. Bertram's final resting place is unknown, but his name is listed on Thiepval Memorial Name listed at Pier and Face 13 C.

Trooper George Ernest Dawkes
145992 - Northamptonshire Yeomanry
Killed 30th October 1918 aged 28.
Buried at: Tezze British Cemetery in Italy, Plot 6. Row A. Grave 6.

Private Adolphus Jack
6949 - British West Indies Regiment 1st Battalion
Around 22nd June 1918 was admitted to No.81 General Hospital for War Related Sickness, but died age 26.
Remembered on: Hollybrook Memorial, Southampton.

Lance Corporal Norman Frank Ewart Cole
10406 - Oxford and Bucks Light Infantry
Killed: 15th September 1916 aged 23.
Killed at the Battle of Flers-Courcelette and is Remembered on: Thiepval Memorial Pier.

Rifleman Henry William Longman
R/32294 - King’s Royal Rifle Corps
Killed: 24th October 1917 aged 27.
Killed by a shell which burst fairly near him, and overcome by concussion Flanders France. Remembered on the Tyne Cot Memorial, Panel 115 to 119.
